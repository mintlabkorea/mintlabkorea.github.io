Robot Intelligence
A hallmark of robotic intelligence is the ability to learn and act through perception. Vision-based learning enables robots to contextualize the world beyond the capabilities of large language models by leveraging multimodal information through Visual Language Models (VLMs). Additional sensing modalities, such as tactile sensors and inertial measurement units, can be integrated to provide richer representations of interactions with environments and humans. From a low-level control perspective, combining traditional control theory with reinforcement learning enables more dexterous locomotion and manipulation. Our lab aims to advance the capabilities of intelligent robots by exploring, among other topics, vision-language-action models, multi-agent reinforcement learning, computer vision, and quantum reinforcement learning.

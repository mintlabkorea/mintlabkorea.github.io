Wearable Intelligence
Research on wearable sensors is driven by their potential to enable continuous monitoring of physiological and biomechanical signals in everyday settings. Wearable devices offer captures of motion-related data, which can be used in various circumstances. Combining the given data with Artificial Intelligence (AI), the collected data can be used in body pose estimation, and status tracking under a certain context. Such capabilities are particularly valuable in applications involving human movement analysis and adaptive control systems. In the context of assistive robotics, wearable sensors play a critical role in the operation of exoskeletons by providing real-time feedback to interpret the userâ€™s motion intent and support coordinated movement. This enhances the effectiveness of mobility assistance, rehabilitation, or physical augmentation. Additionally, the rich data generated by wearable sensors contributes to more accurate modeling of human behavior and supports the development of context-aware systems.
